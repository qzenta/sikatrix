SARS has confirmed the 2026 filing season dates. For most South African taxpayers, the season opens on 1 July 2026 with auto assessments, followed by a broader manual filing period that runs through to January 2027 for those with more complex tax affairs. Whether you are a salaried employee, a freelancer, or a business owner, here is everything you need to know before the season opens.

## Filing Season 2026: Key Dates at a Glance

**1 to 12 July 2026: Auto Assessments**

SARS will issue auto assessments to non-provisional taxpayers whose income is straightforward, typically salaried employees with a single employer and PAYE fully deducted throughout the year. SARS expects to issue approximately 6 million auto assessments during this window.

If you receive an auto assessment and agree with it, no further action is required. SARS will process your refund or collect your payment automatically. If you disagree, you have 40 business days to submit a manual return and override the assessment. Ignoring a wrong auto assessment means it becomes final.

**13 July to 23 October 2026: Non-Provisional Taxpayers**

From 13 July, taxpayers who need to file manually can do so through eFiling or the SARS MobiApp. This includes anyone with more than one income source, rental income, investments, or any income not fully covered by employer PAYE.

**Provisional Taxpayers and Trusts: Until 22 January 2027**

Provisional taxpayers (most business owners, freelancers, contractors, and investors) and trusts have until 22 January 2027 to file their ITR12 returns. However, your first provisional tax return (IRP6) for the year ending February 2027 is due by 31 August 2026, so the season involves two parallel processes.

## What Is a Non-Provisional Taxpayer?

Non-provisional taxpayers are individuals who earn a regular salary with PAYE deducted by their employer throughout the year. They either receive an auto assessment or submit one return per year. They have no business income, rental income, or significant investment returns outside of what their employer reports.

If your income comes entirely from a salary and you have no side earnings, you are most likely a non-provisional taxpayer.

## What Is a Provisional Taxpayer?

Provisional taxpayers earn income beyond a salary. This includes income from a business, freelancing, consulting, rental properties, dividends, or investments that exceed R30,000 per year. If you fall into this category, you submit two IRP6 returns per year (in August and February) as advance payments, and then reconcile with your ITR12 after the tax year ends.

Most business owners and directors are provisional taxpayers. If you are unsure, your accountant can confirm your registration status with SARS.

## How to Access Your Auto Assessment

SARS will notify you by SMS or email once your auto assessment is available. You must then log in to eFiling at sars.gov.za or use the SARS MobiApp to view and confirm your assessment. Do not click any links in the SMS or email, as fraudsters use fake SARS notifications ahead of filing season to steal credentials. Always navigate directly to sars.gov.za or open the MobiApp yourself.

A virtual waiting room will be active on both platforms during the 1 to 12 July rush to manage high traffic volumes.

## What Is New in 2026

SARS has updated the ITR12 return for the 2026 season with a new layout designed to make filing faster and reduce errors. Key changes include more pre-filled taxpayer information drawn from employer IRP5 data, medical scheme records, and bank submissions. There is also a new drop-down list for medical aid selection to reduce manual entry mistakes, quick links to your Notice of Assessment, and a new declaration alert that flags potential issues before you submit.

You can now receive your Notice of Assessment (ITA34), your Statement of Account, and submit supporting documents via WhatsApp. This is a significant improvement for taxpayers who prefer mobile access.

## When to See a Tax Practitioner Before Filing

Not every return benefits from professional help, but some situations warrant it. You should speak to a registered tax practitioner before filing if you:

- Have income from multiple sources
- Run a business and are registered for provisional tax
- Received a large bonus, commission, or once-off payment in the year
- Have unreported income from previous years
- Received an auto assessment you believe is incorrect
- Have received any correspondence from SARS that you have not responded to

In these situations, filing incorrectly or ignoring an auto assessment can result in penalties, interest, and a SARS audit.

## How to Book an Appointment with SARS

If you need to visit a SARS Service Centre in person, all branches are fully staffed on weekdays from 08:00 to 16:00. You can book an appointment in three ways: online via the SARS website, by calling 0800 00 7277 and selecting option 0, or by SMS, by sending the word "Booking" followed by your ID or passport number to 47277.
