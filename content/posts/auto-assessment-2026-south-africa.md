From 1 July 2026, SARS will begin issuing auto assessments to millions of South African taxpayers. While this automated process is designed to simplify filing for straightforward tax affairs, business owners and provisional taxpayers sit outside the auto assessment window and face a separate set of obligations. Understanding the difference between who qualifies and who does not is essential to staying compliant this filing season.

## What Is an Auto Assessment?

An auto assessment is a tax return that SARS pre-populates and issues on your behalf, using data submitted by third parties: your employer, your bank, your medical scheme, your retirement annuity fund, and other institutions. SARS uses this data to calculate what it believes you owe or are owed, and issues an assessment without requiring you to file manually.

If the assessment is correct and you accept it, no further action is required. SARS processes your refund or payment automatically. If it is incorrect, you have 40 business days to file a manual return to override it.

## Who Gets an Auto Assessment in 2026?

Auto assessments are issued only to non-provisional taxpayers whose income is simple enough for SARS to process automatically. This typically means:

- Employees with a single employer whose PAYE has been fully deducted throughout the year
- Individuals whose only additional income is interest below R23,800 (under 65) or R34,500 (65 and older)
- Taxpayers whose medical aid contributions and retirement annuity contributions are already on record with SARS via certificates

SARS expects to issue approximately 6 million auto assessments between 1 and 12 July 2026. A virtual waiting room will be active on eFiling and the SARS MobiApp to manage volume surges during this period.

## What Is New in the 2026 ITR12

The ITR12 return has been updated with a new look and feel for easier navigation. Key changes include more pre-filled taxpayer information, a new drop-down list for medical aid selection to reduce input errors, quick links to your Notice of Assessment (ITA34), and clearer messages when a return is overdue. A new declaration alert feature has been added to identify potential issues before you submit.

These improvements reduce admin time for taxpayers who do file manually, but they do not change the underlying obligation to file correctly and by the deadline.

## Who Does NOT Get an Auto Assessment

This is where it matters most for business owners. The following categories of taxpayer do not receive auto assessments and must file manually from 13 July 2026:

- Provisional taxpayers: anyone with business income, freelance income, rental income, investment income exceeding the threshold, or any income source beyond a single employer
- Business owners and directors
- Trusts, which have a separate deadline of 22 January 2027
- Anyone with more than one employer in the tax year
- Taxpayers with foreign income or employment

If you run a business, even a part-time one alongside salaried employment, you are almost certainly a provisional taxpayer. Your ITR12 deadline is 22 January 2027, but your first IRP6 provisional tax payment for the 2026/27 year is due on 31 August 2026, well before the ITR12 window closes.

## Common Mistakes That Trigger Manual Review

Even taxpayers who receive an auto assessment can find themselves in difficulty if the pre-filled data is wrong. The most common problems we see include:

- Outdated banking details at SARS, which delays refunds or causes failed debit orders
- Incorrect employer IRP5 data, where the IRP5 submitted by your employer does not match what you actually earned
- Missing medical aid contributions, where the certificate submitted by your scheme does not reflect the full year
- Unclaimed retirement annuity contributions, where contributions are not reflected on the auto assessment because the fund did not submit them
- Rental income and investment returns that were not reported to SARS by the relevant institution

As the taxpayer, you remain legally responsible for the accuracy of your return. An incorrect auto assessment that you accepted is still your liability if SARS audits you later.

## What to Do If You Are a Business Owner

If you are registered as a provisional taxpayer, your tax calendar for the second half of 2026 includes:

1. **31 August 2026**: First IRP6 provisional tax return, estimating your taxable income for the year ending 28 February 2027
2. **22 January 2027**: ITR12 annual return for the year ending 28 February 2026 (the completed tax year)
3. **28 February 2027**: Second IRP6 provisional tax return

Getting your estimates right on the IRP6 reduces the risk of underpayment penalties and a large top-up payment at year-end. If your business has performed significantly better or worse than expected, that should be reflected in your provisional estimate.

## What Records You Will Need

For your ITR12, have the following ready before filing:

- IRP5 or IT3(a) certificates from all income sources
- Medical scheme contribution certificates
- Retirement annuity fund contribution certificates
- Records of rental income and associated expenses
- Provisional tax payment confirmations (IRP6 receipts)
- Business income and expense records if filing as a sole proprietor

If your bookkeeping is not current, now is the time to fix that before the filing window opens.
